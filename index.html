Bhagat Singh

1907-1931

Shaheed-e-Azam

Shaheed-e-Azam Sardar Bhagat Singh was born on 28 September 1907 in the village of 105 Chak Banga, located in the Jarranwala tehsil of Lyallpur District (now Faisalabad) in present-day Pakistan. His father Sardar Kishan Singh Sandhu and mother Vidyavati, raised Bhagat Singh along with his five brothers and three sisters. After being sent to the village School in Banga for a few years, Bhagat Singh was enrolled in the Dayanand Anglo-Vedic School in Lahore. He also worked as a writer and editor in Amritsar for Punjabi and Urdu-language newspapers espousing Marxist theories. Bhagat Singh was a brave freedom fighter in India. He was only 23 when the British rulers hanged him. His sacrifice made him a hero in India’s fight against British rule.Bhagat Singh is often called "Shaheed Bhagat Singh" with respect. He strongly believed in Socialism, thinking it was the way to change Society for the better. He is one of the most Ionic and famous figures of the Independence of India
